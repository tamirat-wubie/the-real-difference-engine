# Evidence Rules

The Real Difference Engine separates fact, inference, speculation, and judgment.

This protects trust.

## Fact

A fact is a verifiable claim.

Use facts when discussing:

- science
- medicine
- law
- finance
- politics
- current events
- companies
- platform policies
- historical events

## Inference

An inference is reasoning from facts.

Example:

Fact:

Audience members give attention.

Inference:

Attention can become income only when trust, need, offer, and value align.

## Speculation

Speculation is possible but not proven.

Use clear language:

- may
- could
- possible
- likely
- uncertain

## Judgment

Judgment is the final interpretation.

Example:

Audience gives attention. Customer gives payment.

## Rule for Risky Domains

For finance, law, medicine, politics, religion, active conflicts, or public claims about real people:

- verify facts
- cite sources when publishing long-form content
- separate fact from interpretation
- avoid personal professional advice unless qualified
- avoid overconfident claims

## Public Safety Statement

The Real Difference is an educational comparison and reasoning platform. It provides conceptual analysis, not professional legal, medical, financial, tax, or investment advice.
