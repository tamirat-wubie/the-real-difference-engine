# The 10-Lens Method for Comparing Anything

Most people compare things too quickly.

They ask:

**Which is better?**

A deeper question is:

**Better through what lens?**

A lens is the dimension of comparison.

The same two things can produce different truths under different lenses.

## 1. Power Lens

Ask:

Which has more force, control, leverage, or influence?

Example:

Hulk vs Thanos through usable power.

Final truth:

Hulk is the greater engine. Thanos is the greater system.

## 2. Time Lens

Ask:

Which wins short-term, long-term, or over repeated cycles?

Example:

Motivation vs Discipline through time.

Final truth:

Motivation starts motion. Discipline preserves motion.

## 3. Meaning Lens

Ask:

What does each thing symbolize?

## 4. Transformation Lens

Ask:

How does each thing change the world?

Example:

Fire vs Language through transformation.

Final truth:

Fire transforms matter. Language transforms meaning.

## 5. Trust Lens

Ask:

Which creates, stores, or destroys confidence?

Example:

Brand vs Reputation through trust memory.

Final truth:

Brand is the promise. Reputation is the receipt.

## 6. Economics Lens

Ask:

Which creates, captures, stores, or transfers value?

Example:

Audience vs Customer through income reality.

Final truth:

An audience gives attention. A customer gives payment.

## 7. Survival Lens

Ask:

Which persists better under pressure?

## 8. Emotion Lens

Ask:

How does each thing regulate feeling or behavior?

## 9. Information Lens

Ask:

How does each thing handle knowledge, signals, or decisions?

## 10. Failure Lens

Ask:

How does each thing break?

## Final Method

Use this sentence:

`A vs B through lens L inside context C.`

Then ask:

1. What is A at the root?
2. What is B at the root?
3. What chain produces A's behavior?
4. What chain produces B's behavior?
5. What do they secretly share?
6. What truly separates them?
7. When does A win?
8. When does B win?
9. What useful truth appears?
10. What is the final memorable line?

## Closing Line

Everything can be compared when the lens is clear.
