# Chapter 2 - The Lens Rule

The most important rule in deep comparison is simple:

**No lens, no comparison.**

A comparison without a lens becomes random.

For example:

**Fire vs language**

At first, this sounds strange. Fire is physical. Language is symbolic.

But add a lens:

**Fire vs language through transformation**

Now the comparison becomes meaningful.

Fire transforms matter.  
Language transforms meaning.

The lens tells the mind what dimension to compare.

Before comparing anything, write:

`A vs B through lens L inside context C.`
