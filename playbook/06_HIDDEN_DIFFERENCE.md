# Chapter 6 - Hidden Difference

A hidden difference is the real separation beneath the surface.

It answers:

**What makes A and B operate differently, even when they appear similar?**

Example:

Audience vs Customer

Surface similarity:

Both are groups of people connected to your work.

Hidden difference:

**Audience gives attention. Customer gives payment.**

Types:

1. Driver difference
2. Time difference
3. Scale difference
4. Failure difference
5. Control difference
6. Reality-layer difference

A good hidden difference should change how the viewer thinks.
